Academic Code of Honor

Make Good Choices - Live Life with Integrity

Talbert Middle School considers high ethical and moral behavior in education essential. Individual students are responsible for their own work and actions. Therefore, the following Academic Code of Honor is in effect at Talbert Middle School.

Students will be responsible for honest test taking practices by:not receiving or providing information before or during a test,not using unauthorized materials on a test,not violating testing rules as outlined by the teacher.
Students shall be responsible for their own work and shall not allow other students to use or copy it unless permitted by the teacher.
Students shall complete written work without using ideas or written material from other sources (books, magazines, internet), unless proper credit is given to avoid plagiarism.
Students shall obtain valid parent signatures on all school documents and papers that require such. Forgery is a serious offense.
This list is not meant to be all-inclusive. Other actions not specifically contained herein may also rightfully be conceived as dishonest.

Discipline

The following procedures will be followed when a student is found in violation of this Academic Code of Honor.

1st Offense: Student receives an “F” or zero on the assignment or test, a lowered citizenship grade, and a disciplinary referral (the referral will follow Talbert’s progressive disciplinary policy).

2nd Offense: Same as the 1st offense plus an automatic Saturday School or equivalent detentions.

3rd Offense: Same as the 1st offense plus an automatic one-day suspension.
